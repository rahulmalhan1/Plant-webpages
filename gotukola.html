Gotu Kola

(Centella asiatica)

What is Gotu Kola?

Gotu Kola, or Centella asiatica, is a herbaceous, perennial plant in the carrot family (Apiaceae). It is native to wetlands in Asia and is revered in Ayurvedic, traditional Chinese, and Indonesian medicine. It is often called the "herb of longevity" and is a staple in many traditional health systems for its cognitive and skin benefits.

Botanical Description

Gotu Kola is a low-growing, creeping plant. It has slender stems and small, rounded to fan-shaped green leaves with scalloped edges. It spreads by sending out runners (stolons) that root at the nodes. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous white or pinkish-red flowers. It thrives in and around water and in moist, tropical climates.

Traditional & Medicinal Uses

Gotu Kola is considered a powerful rejuvenating herb in Ayurveda, known for its ability to balance all three doshas.

Key Traditional Benefits:

  • Cognitive Support: Famously known as a "brain tonic," it is traditionally used to enhance memory, concentration, and cognitive function.
  • Skin Health & Wound Healing: It is widely used to promote wound healing, reduce scarring, and improve skin conditions like psoriasis and eczema.
  • Stress & Anxiety Reduction: Gotu Kola has calming properties and is used as an adaptogen to help the body cope with stress and anxiety.
  • Circulation: It is believed to support healthy blood circulation and vein health.

How to Grow Gotu Kola

This plant is relatively easy to grow if its moisture needs are met.

  • Sunlight: Prefers partial shade but can tolerate full sun if the soil is kept consistently wet.
  • Soil: Needs fertile, moist to wet soil. It can even be grown as a shallow aquatic plant.
  • Watering: This is the key. The soil must never be allowed to dry out. It is an excellent plant for boggy areas or container water gardens.
  • Propagation: Spreads easily via its runners, which can be divided and replanted.
