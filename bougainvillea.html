Bougainvillea

(Bougainvillea glabra / Bougainvillea spectabilis)

What is Bougainvillea?

Bougainvillea is a genus of thorny, ornamental vines, bushes, and trees. It is a tropical, evergreen plant native to South America. It is famous for its "flowers," which are not true flowers at all. The vibrant color comes from three large, papery bracts (modified leaves) that surround three small, inconspicuous white or yellow tubular flowers.

Botanical Description

Bougainvillea is a woody, climbing vine that can grow up to 12 meters (40 ft) tall, scrambling over other plants with its sharp, waxy thorns. The leaves are simple, oval-shaped, and green. The explosion of color—pink, magenta, purple, red, orange, or white—comes entirely from the paper-thin bracts that appear in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Ornamental Use

Bougainvillea is used almost exclusively for its ornamental value.

Key Uses:

  • Landscaping: Its main use is as a stunning, colorful climbing vine to cover walls, fences, and trellises.
  • Drought-Tolerant: Because it thrives in hot, dry conditions, it is a perfect plant for xeriscaping in warm climates.
  • Container Plant: Dwarf varieties are popular as potted plants and bonsai.

How to Grow Bougainvillea

This plant thrives on neglect and loves heat.

  • Sunlight: Requires absolute full sun (at least 6-8 hours a day) to bloom well. It will not bloom in shade.
  • Climate: Needs a hot, dry climate. It is not frost-tolerant.
  • Soil: Prefers well-drained, slightly acidic soil. It does not like "wet feet."
  • Watering: Water deeply, then let the soil dry out completely. Bougainvillea blooms best when it is slightly water-stressed.
