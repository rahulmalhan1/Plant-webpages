Neem

(Azadirachta indica)

What is Neem?

Neem is a fast-growing evergreen tree in the mahogany family, Meliaceae. It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is often called the "village pharmacy" because every part of the tree—leaves, bark, seeds, fruit, and flowers—has been used in traditional Ayurvedic and Unani medicine for thousands of years.

Botanical Description

The neem tree can grow to 15–20 meters (50–65 ft) tall. It has wide, spreading branches and a dense crown of bright green, pinnate leaves (leaves made of many leaflets). It produces small, fragrant white flowers and a small, olive-like, yellow-green fruit (a drupe). The tree is very drought-resistant.

Ayurvedic & Medicinal Uses

Neem is celebrated for its powerful antiseptic, antifungal, antiviral, and antibacterial properties.

Key Uses:

  • Natural Pesticide: Neem oil, pressed from the seeds, is a potent, non-toxic pesticide used in organic farming.
  • Skin Care: Widely used in soaps, lotions, and shampoos to treat skin conditions like acne, eczema, and dandruff.
  • Dental Health: Neem twigs ("datun") have been used for centuries in India as natural toothbrushes due to their antimicrobial properties.
  • Blood Purifier: In Ayurveda, neem is considered a bitter tonic and a powerful blood purifier.

Cultivation

Neem is a hardy tree valued in arid regions.

  • Climate: Thrives in hot, dry, tropical, and subtropical climates.
  • Sunlight: Requires full, direct sunlight.
  • Soil: Tolerates a wide variety of soils, including poor, rocky, and sandy soil. It does not tolerate cold, frosty weather or waterlogged soil.
  • Drought-Tolerant: Exceptionally resistant to drought.
