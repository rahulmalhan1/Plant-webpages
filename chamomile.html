Chamomile

(Matricaria chamomilla)

What is Chamomile?

Chamomile is the common name for several daisy-like plants from the family Asteraceae. The two most common types are German Chamomile (Matricaria chamomilla) and Roman Chamomile (Chamaemelum nobile). It is one of the most ancient and widely used medicinal herbs in the world, best known for its use in herbal tea.

Botanical Description

German Chamomile is an annual plant with thin, feathery, branching stems. It produces small flowers with a yellow, cone-shaped center (receptacle) surrounded by delicate white petals. The flowers have a strong, aromatic, and slightly sweet, apple-like scent. Roman Chamomile is a low-growing perennial, often used as a ground cover.

Traditional & Medicinal Uses

The dried flowers of chamomile are used to make its famous tea.

Key Uses:

  • Calming & Sleep Aid: Chamomile tea is most famous for its mild sedative properties, commonly used to reduce anxiety, calm the nerves, and promote sleep.
  • Digestive Soother: It is also used to help soothe an upset stomach, reduce gas, and relieve symptoms of indigestion.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Can be applied topically in creams or rinses to soothe skin irritations and rashes.
  • Hair Care: A chamomile rinse is sometimes used to gently lighten blonde hair.

How to Grow Chamomile

Chamomile is easy to grow from seed.

  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun but will tolerate light shade.
  • Soil: Does well in average, well-drained soil. It is not a heavy feeder.
  • Planting: Sow seeds directly on the soil surface as they need light to germinate. German chamomile often self-seeds readily.
  • Harvesting: Harvest the flowers when they are fully open, then dry them for tea.
