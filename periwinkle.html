Madagascar Periwinkle

(Catharanthus roseus)

What is Catharanthus roseus?

Catharanthus roseus, commonly known as the Madagascar Periwinkle, Rosy Periwinkle, or Vinca, is a species of flowering plant in the family Apocynaceae. Though it is a popular ornamental garden plant, it is most famous as the source of powerful alkaloids used in cancer chemotherapy.

Botanical Description

The plant is an evergreen subshrub or herbaceous plant. It has glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs. It blooms profusely with simple, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink, mauve, or white, often with a darker red or pink center.

Ornamental & Major Medicinal Uses

While a common garden annual, this plant is a medical marvel.

Key Uses:

  • Ornamental Plant: Widely grown in gardens (especially in hanging baskets and as bedding plants) for its long-lasting, colorful flowers and heat tolerance.
  • Chemotherapy Drugs: This plant is the source of the vinca alkaloids vincristine and vinblastine. These compounds are used in chemotherapy to treat several types of cancer, including leukemia and lymphoma.
  • Traditional Medicine: It was used in various folk remedies, but it is toxic if ingested and should not be consumed.

How to Grow Periwinkle

This is a very easy-to-grow, heat-loving annual (or perennial in warm climates).

  • Sunlight: Thrives in full sun to partial shade.
  • Soil: Prefers well-drained soil. It is very tolerant of poor soil.
  • Watering: Drought-tolerant once established. Let the soil dry out slightly between waterings.
  • Heat-Tolerant: It is one of the best flowers for hot, dry summer weather.
