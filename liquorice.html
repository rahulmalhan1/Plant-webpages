Liquorice

(Glycyrrhiza glabra)

What is Liquorice?

Liquorice (or licorice) is the root of Glycyrrhiza glabra, a herbaceous perennial legume native to Western Asia, North Africa, and Southern Europe. The plant is prized for its sweet-tasting root, which is used as a flavoring in candy and beverages, and as a powerful herb in traditional medicine.

The most valuable part is its extensive root system, which consists of a long, woody main taproot and many spreading runners (stolons).

Traditional & Medicinal Uses

The sweetness of liquorice root comes from glycyrrhizin, a compound significantly sweeter than sugar.

Key Uses:

  • Flavoring: Used to make liquorice candy, and to flavor beverages like root beer, as well as some savory dishes.
  • Digestive Health: A very common traditional remedy for soothing stomach ulcers, heartburn, and acid reflux.
  • Respiratory Health: Used as an expectorant to help relieve coughs and clear mucus.
  • Adrenal Support: Believed to have properties that support adrenal gland function.

Note: Liquorice should be consumed in moderation, as excessive intake of glycyrrhizin can lead to side effects like high blood pressure.

Cultivation

Liquorice is a hardy plant but requires patience.

  • Sunlight: Prefers full sun.
  • Soil: Needs deep, sandy, well-drained soil to allow its large root system to develop.
  • Harvesting: The roots are not harvested until the plants are 3-5 years old to allow them to mature and develop their sweet flavor.
