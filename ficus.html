Ficus (Fig)

(Ficus sp.)

What is Ficus?

Ficus is a very large genus of about 850 species of woody trees, shrubs, and vines in the mulberry family (Moraceae). It is native to the tropics, with a few species extending into temperate zones. The genus includes the Common Fig (Ficus carica), giant Banyan trees (like Ficus benghalensis), and popular houseplants like the Weeping Fig (Ficus benjamina) and the Fiddle-leaf Fig (Ficus lyrata).

Uses & Significance

The Ficus genus is significant both ecologically and economically.

Key Uses:

  • Food: The Common Fig (Ficus carica) is cultivated for its sweet, edible fruit.
  • Houseplants: Ficus benjamina (Weeping Fig), Ficus lyrata (Fiddle-leaf Fig), and Ficus elastica (Rubber Plant) are extremely popular indoor ornamental plants.
  • Cultural: The Bodhi tree (Ficus religiosa) is sacred in Buddhism as the tree under which the Buddha attained enlightenment. Banyan trees are also sacred in Hinduism.
  • Ecological: Figs are a keystone species in many tropical forests, providing a year-round food source for fruit-eating animals.

How to Grow Ficus (Indoors)

As houseplants, Ficus trees (like Weeping Fig) are popular but notoriously fussy.

  • Sunlight: Need bright, indirect light. Direct sun can scorch the leaves.
  • Watering: Water thoroughly when the top few inches of soil are dry. They do not like to be consistently wet or to dry out completely.
  • Stability: Ficus trees hate to be moved. They are known for dropping many leaves in protest after a change in location, light, or temperature.
