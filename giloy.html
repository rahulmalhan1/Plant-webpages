Giloy

(Tinospora cordifolia)

What is Giloy?

Giloy, also known as Amrita (which translates to "the root of immortality" in Sanskrit), is a climbing shrub that is highly esteemed in Ayurveda for its potent medicinal properties. It is a herbaceous vine belonging to the family Menispermaceae and is native to the tropical regions of the Indian subcontinent. The stem of the Giloy plant is the most widely used part.

Botanical Description

Giloy is a large, deciduous climbing shrub. Its most recognizable feature is its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant has a succulent stem, which is creamy-white to greyish-brown and covered in a thin, papery bark. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers and green, pea-sized fruits that turn bright red when ripe.

Ayurvedic & Medicinal Uses

In Ayurveda, Giloy is considered a Rasayana (rejuvenator) and is used to treat a wide range of health conditions. It is known as a tridoshic herb, meaning it helps balance all three doshas (Vata, Pitta, and Kapha).

Key Traditional Benefits:

  • Immune Booster: Giloy is a powerhouse of antioxidants and is famous for its ability to boost the immune system and fight off infections.
  • Anti-pyretic (Fever Reducer): It is traditionally used to treat chronic fevers, including those associated with dengue and malaria.
  • Manages Diabetes: It helps in managing blood sugar levels by improving insulin sensitivity.
  • Anti-inflammatory: Its anti-inflammatory properties are beneficial in treating conditions like arthritis.
  • Stress Reduction: Like Tulsi, Giloy is also considered an adaptogen, helping to reduce mental stress and anxiety.

How to Grow Giloy

Giloy is a hardy plant and can be easily grown at home.

  • Propagation: It is most easily grown from stem cuttings.
  • Support: As a climber, it needs a trellis, wall, or a host tree to grow on. It is often seen climbing neem or mango trees.
  • Soil: It adapts to most soil types but prefers well-drained soil.
  • Watering: It requires moderate watering.
